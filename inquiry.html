Art as Inquiry and Knowledge-Making

My work explores how artistic practice can function as a mode of inquiry and reflection. Through experimentation, iterative making, and systematic documentation of the creative processes, I investigate how art-making can foster curiosity, support learning, and generate new insights into scientific and health ideas.

Art Meets Science: A Celebration of Inquiry

Supervisors: Mr Chia Wen Hou and Ms Joanna Ng

Raffles Institution, Singapore

This coursework investigates the intersection of art and science, challenging the assumed polarity between the two fields. Through experimental exploration using diverse media, the project explores how both disciplines share similar processes of inquiry—experimentation, observation, and iterative discovery.


The final installation resembles a hybrid between an artist’s studio and a research laboratory, displaying sketches, experimental drafts, and material explorations. Rather than presenting a single finished outcome, the work foregrounds the iterative and investigative process behind creative and scientific discovery. By highlighting experimentation, curiosity, and obsessive hands-on practice, the project demonstrates how artistic experimentation can generate insight, and bridge creative and scientific inquiry.

Sketchbook Discoveries: A Collection of Experimentations and Insights

By highlighting experimentation, curiosity, and obsessive hands-on practice, the project demonstrates how artistic experimentation can generate insight, and bridge creative and scientific inquiry.


Rather than presenting only finished works, the artbook foregrounds the process of artistic inquiry—how experimentation, reflection, and revision contribute to understanding and discovery. It serves as a visual record of how my practice evolved, showing the interplay between trial, error, and insight across multiple projects. Through this systematic documentation, the artbook demonstrates how art-making can function as a method of inquiry, generating knowledge and deepening understanding in both creative and scientific contexts.
