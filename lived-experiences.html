Art for Conveying Lived Experiences

My work explores how creative practices can convey the nuanced lived experiences of illness and health. Through collaborative and participatory projects, I investigate how art can amplify patient voices and foster dialogue around patient perspectives in healthcare.

The Artist’s Residency: A collaborative exploration of lived experiences of mental disorders

In collaboration with Mental Health Collective, Singapore and REACH

This collaborative art project brings together student artists and artists with lived experiences of mental illness. Through creative collaborations, personal experiences are translated into visual artworks that reflect emotional, social, and everyday dimensions of mental disorders. The works were curated into a public exhibition at the National Library, Singapore, inviting audiences to engage with these narratives, foster dialogue, and challenge stigma.


As part of the exhibition, I created an animation inspired by the lived experiences of a dancer navigating depression, anxiety, and an eating disorder. Drawing from the collaborator’s reflections on movement and internal struggle, the animation explores the complex dynamic between the self and the disorder.

Postcard Project: Investigating Lived Experiences of Hereditary Cancer

Led by: Assoc. Prof Joanne Ngeow

Cancer Genetics Service, Division of Medical Oncology

National Cancer Center, Singapore

This ongoing project explores the lived experiences of individuals affected by hereditary cancers, including patients, their families, and healthcare professionals involved in their care. The study uses postcards as a creative medium to capture personal reflections expressed through both visual and written language.


Participants are invited to respond to prompts by illustrating or writing on postcards, allowing them to share their experiences, emotions, and perspectives in ways that extend beyond conventional survey responses. The postcards function as small narrative artefacts, offering insight into how individuals interpret and navigate hereditary cancer in their daily lives. By combining creative expression with qualitative research, this project investigates how visual and narrative forms can document lived experiences, amplify patient voices, and foster reflection within healthcare contexts.
