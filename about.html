About me

Growing up reading Young Scientists and Cells at Work, I became fascinated by visual storytelling as a way to spark curiosity and understanding.


I bring together my background in art, biology, and psychology to explore creative ways of learning, experimenting, and reflecting on scientific and health topics. My past experiences include working as a student research assistant, mentoring students, and teaching rollerblading — all of which have strengthened my skills in communication, guidance, and hands-on learning.


I currently work as a Research Coordinator at the National Cancer Centre Singapore, supporting multidisciplinary projects, where I develop skills in research design, data collection and analysis, academic writing, and project coordination.
